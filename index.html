About Me

Research Interests: Time-domain astronomy, optical imaging surveys, eclipsing white dwarf binaries, machine learning, fast transients.

Publications: ADS.

Education:
PhD Thesis

For my PhD research (also here) at Radboud University, I used the Palomar Transient Factory (PTF) to study the optical variable sky. The first half focusses on fast optical transients. The second half is about eclipsing white dwarf binary systems in the PTF data.

Master thesis

The subject of my Master Thesis is PTF1108ag, a new eclipsing white dwarf - red dwarf binary star. This research was carried out during 2012-2013, the second year of my Master education at the Radboud University Nijmegen under supervision of Paul Groot.

Bachelor project

A the end of my Bachelor education at Radboud University Nijmegen, I did a 3 month project. under supervision of Gijs Nelemans, analysing spectra of pulsating SdO star SDSS J160043.6+074802.9 ( Woudt et al. 2006).
